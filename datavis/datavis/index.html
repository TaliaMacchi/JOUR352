Student loan complaints spiked in the 2010s as borrowers struggled to naviagate services

By: Talia Macchi

Student loan complaints rose through the mid to late 2010s as more Americans took on education debt and struggled to navigate a repayment system many borrowers views as confusing. Texas, Flordia and Califorina saw the highest number of complaints, though those states have the largest populations. Most complaints were about federal loans because that is what most borrowers had. Many of the issues weren't just that people couldn't afford to pay, but that they didn't understand their options or couldn't get help.

Navient argues that the system is complicated and they were just following the rules that the government gave them, which seems to be partly true. However, many complaints were about how the loans were handled -- inaccurate information, mishandled paymenrs and denied borrowers access to lower-cost repayment programs. The broader student loan system clearly played a role in the rise of complaints, but the pattern of complaints shows that borrowers' frustrations were more than just the complexity of the system.
